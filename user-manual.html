Getting Started

🚀Getting Started

Creating an Account

  1. Open the app and tap Sign Up.
  2. Enter your first name, last name, email address, and a password, then tap Next.
  3. You will be asked what you want to do — Create an Organisation or Join an Organisation. Choose the path that applies to you (see below).
Already have an account? Tap Sign In instead and enter your email and password. If you've previously enabled Face ID, the Face ID button will appear on the sign-in screen for one-tap access.

Path A: Creating an Organisation Admin

Choose this if you are the admin — the person setting up the app for a band or company for the first time.

  1. Tap Create an Organisation. A subscription is required — the paywall will appear. Subscribe to unlock admin access.
  2. Once subscribed, enter a name for your organisation (e.g. The Angels or Rock The Boat Management) and tap Create Organisation.
  3. You are signed in and taken straight to the app. Your organisation is ready — you can now add tours, dates, and invite crew.
To invite crew and artists, go to the Admin tab → Invite Codes. Generate an invite code and share it with each person — they sign up using Join an Organisation and enter the code.

Path B: Joining an Organisation

Choose this if you have been invited by an admin — you are a crew member, artist, or tour manager joining an existing organisation.

  1. Tap Join an Organisation.
  2. Enter the invite code your admin has shared with you (e.g. ABC123) and tap Join.
  3. You are signed in and added to the organisation automatically. Your role (Tour Manager, Crew, or Artist) is assigned by the admin.
If you don't have an invite code, ask your admin to generate one from the Admin tab → Invite Codes.

Selecting an Organisation

All tour data is scoped to an organisation. If you belong to more than one, tap the Organisations tab (or go to Settings → Organisations) to switch between them. The active organisation is shown with a blue border and a checkmark. All dates, songs, crew, and contacts update instantly when you switch.

If you manage more than one artist, create a separate organisation for each. Data is completely isolated between organisations — switching never mixes content.

Face ID / Touch ID

After signing in with your email and password for the first time, the app will offer to enable Face ID (or Touch ID on older devices). Tap Enable to save your credentials securely. On future sign-ins, a Sign In with Face ID button appears on the login screen — tap it to authenticate with a glance instead of typing your password.

For security, the app will ask you to re-authenticate (via Face ID or password) if it has been closed for more than 48 hours.

Navigation

The app uses a bottom tab bar with five main areas:

  • Dates (mic icon) — your tour dates list, the main hub
  • Calendar — monthly calendar view of all dates
  • Songs — the master song library
  • Crew — band and crew contacts
  • Notifications (bell icon) — inbox and message sending

Hotel, Backline, Guest List, Transport, Contacts, Admin, and Organisations are accessed from within the relevant screen or via the navigation flow.

Tap the ⋮ menu in the top-right header of any screen to access the following options:

  • ✅ Tasks — open the task list (Admin and Tour Manager only)
  • 📁 Files — shared tour file library
  • 👤 Contacts — venue and tour contacts
  • ⚙️ Settings — appearance, time format, organisations, and admin options (see Settings below)
  • 📖 User Manual — opens this guide in-app
  • 🚪 Sign Out

The header also includes a ↻ refresh button (spinning arrow icon) next to the ⋮ menu. Tap it at any time to reload tour data, the user list, and notifications from the cloud. All screens update immediately.

Offline & Sync

World Tour Pro works offline. All data is cached on your device so you can view and edit tour information without an internet connection. When connectivity is restored, changes sync to the cloud automatically.

A yellow ⚠ Sync Pending banner appears in the app header when local changes haven't yet reached the cloud. Tap Sync Now in the banner to force an immediate upload.

iOS Accessibility — Larger Text

The app header (app name, organisation name, tour selector, and ⋮ menu) is designed at a fixed size and does not scale with the iOS Larger Text accessibility setting (Settings → Accessibility → Display & Text Size → Larger Text). This prevents the header from overflowing on smaller screens. All other text throughout the app — show details, schedules, contacts, crew cards, etc. — does respect the Larger Text setting.

Tour Dates

🎤Tour Dates

The Dates tab is your home screen. It shows all tours and dates for the active year, organised as collapsible group cards.

Tour Year Selector

The app organises all tours and dates by year. A 📅 year button (e.g. 📅 2026 ▼) appears in the header when you're on the Dates or Calendar tab. Tap it to open the Year panel, which lists all available tour years. Tap any year to switch to it — the Dates screen will reload showing only tours for that year.

To add a new year, tap + Add Tour Year at the bottom of the list and enter a 4-digit year.

Your selected year is remembered between sessions. If the dates list looks empty, check that the correct year is selected in the header.

Creating a Tour Admin

Tours (also called groups) are the containers for your show dates within a year. To create one, tap the + button next to the TOURS/DATES heading on the Dates screen. Enter a name (e.g. World Tour 2026) and tap Add. The new tour card appears immediately under the active year.

You can have as many tours as you need within a year — one per leg, one per artist, etc. All dates, hotels, transport, and files are stored per-tour.

Tour Group Cards

Each tour is displayed as a collapsible group card with two rows:

  • Row 1: coloured dot  ·  tour name
  • Row 2: date count  ·  ✏ edit  ·  + Date  ·  share PDF  ·  🗑 delete  ·  expand ∨ chevron

Tap the chevron to expand or collapse the dates within that tour.

The Ungrouped card (if present) shows dates that have been moved out of their original tour. It has no management controls — only the expand chevron and a date count. See Ungrouped Dates below.

Reordering Tours Admin

Tap Edit next to the TOURS/DATES heading to enter reorder mode. A ≡ drag handle appears on the left of each tour card. Long-press the handle and drag the card to its new position. Tap Done to save the new order.

Editing a Tour Name & Colour Admin

Tap the ✏ pencil icon on a tour card to open the Edit Tour modal. Update the tour name, choose one of the 8 colour swatches, then tap Save. The tour's coloured dot updates immediately throughout the app.

Deleting a Tour Admin

Tap the trash icon on a tour card. You will be asked to confirm before anything is removed.

A tour can only be deleted when it has no dates. If dates are still present, a blocking alert will appear: "[Tour name]" still has X dates. Move or delete all dates before removing this tour. Use the ⋮ menu on each date to move them to another group first.

Ungrouped Dates Admin

Ungrouped is a special holding area for dates that have been moved out of their original tour but not yet assigned to another. It appears as a grey card below all named tours, and only when it contains at least one date.

  • To move a date into Ungrouped: tap the ⋮ menu on the date → Move to Group → Ungrouped.
  • To move a date out of Ungrouped: tap the ⋮ menu on the date → Move to Group → choose a named tour.

The Ungrouped card has no add, edit, share, or delete controls — it is a temporary home only. Once all its dates are moved away, the card disappears automatically.

Filter Bar

A row of filter controls sits above the tour cards:

  • All / Show / Travel / Off — filter dates by day type
  • Today (green pill) — opens the Show Screen for today's date. If today has no booked date, an alert displays "Nothing booked. Enjoy your day off! 🌞"
  • Sort ↑↓ — toggle ascending or descending date order
  • Hide past / Show all — show or hide past dates across all tours
  • Timeline / Hide timeline (blue pill) — toggle the flat chronological Timeline view (see below)
If the list appears empty, check whether past dates are hidden. The filter bar shows how many are concealed — tap Show all to reveal them.

Timeline View

Tap Timeline in the filter bar to reveal a flat chronological list of all dates below the group cards, regardless of which tour they belong to. Each row shows:

  • A coloured left border matching the tour's group colour
  • The tour name as a small label
  • The date, venue, and city

Month headers separate different months as you scroll. All active filters (day type, hide past, search) apply to the timeline too. Tap Hide timeline to collapse it.

Reading the Dates List

Within an expanded tour card, dates are listed chronologically. Each row shows a calendar block (day, date, month) on the left, the venue name as main text, and the city below it. A small eye icon indicates the date has restricted visibility. Tap any date to open the full Show Screen.

Adding a Date Admin

  1. Tap the + Date button on the tour card (Row 2) for the tour you want to add the date to.
  2. Enter the date (dd/mm/yyyy), city, and venue. Typing in the venue field shows Google Places suggestions — select one to auto-fill the address, city, postcode, country, and timezone.
  3. Tap Add Date to save.

Editing or Deleting a Date Admin

Long-press a date row to reveal inline edit (pencil) and delete (trash) buttons for that row. Tap the pencil to open the Edit Date form (same fields as Add Date). Tap the trash to delete the date with a confirmation prompt.

Sharing a Tour as PDF Admin Tour Manager

Tap the share icon on a tour card (Row 2) to generate a PDF of all dates in that tour. A full-screen preview opens — scroll through the document before sharing. Tap Share at the bottom to send via AirDrop, email, or any other share option.

The PDF includes:

  • Organisation name as the document header
  • All dates formatted as Fri 14 Mar 2026 with venue and city
  • The shared date and World Tour Pro as a footer

The document is formatted to A4 size with standard print margins.

📋Show Screen

The Show Screen is the central hub for a single show day. Tap any date from the Dates tab to open it.

Header — Venue, Date & City

The venue name appears as a large text field at the top. Admin users can tap it to edit and see Google Places autocomplete suggestions. The date and city fields are shown below and are also editable by admins. A timezone row shows the venue's local time (updated every minute).

Undo a deletion: After deleting a date, a toast slides up from the bottom of the screen for 5 seconds. Tap Undo to restore the date instantly. On a physical device you can also shake the device within those 5 seconds to trigger the same undo.

A row of five buttons gives fast access to linked screens:

  • Hotel — hotel name, address, and room assignments

Opens full-screen. Contains fields for:

Schedule

A timeline showing all schedule items and transport entries for the day, sorted in chronological order (earliest first). Items show a time on the left and an activity name on the right, connected by a coloured dot and line. Items with both a start and end time display the time as a range — e.g. 20:15–21:45.

Times are displayed in whichever format is set in ⚙️ Settings → Time Format (12 hr or 24 hr). Transport entries in the timeline show the party name above the depart/arrive line.

Admin Tour Manager can tap the + button in the Schedule header to open the dedicated Schedule editing screen. From there:

  • Tap + Add Item to add a new schedule item (Activity Name, Start Time, End Time)
  • Edit the activity name or times for any existing item — changes are saved automatically
  • Delete a schedule item with the trash button — a confirmation prompt is shown before the item is removed

Items are always listed in chronological order on the Schedule screen (earliest start time first).

Time input is always in 24-hour format (e.g. 17:00 for 5 PM). Times are displayed to all users in their chosen format (12 hr or 24 hr), but must be entered as HH:MM in 24-hour time.

Transport items in the timeline are read-only — tap them to go to the Transport screen. Flight entries display the party name and flight number as a tappable blue link ↗ — tap the flight number to open a Google search for live flight status in your browser.

Ground transport arrive entries display the arrival location as a tappable blue link ↗ — tap it to open Google Maps with that address for directions.

Songs that have a Print Title set in the Song Library will display that title instead of the song name in the set list on screen, in PDF and text exports, and in the Day Sheet. Songs with a print title show it in blue beneath the song name in the Song Library.

Break rows appear as a horizontal divider labelled — Break — and can be repositioned by long-pressing and dragging, just like songs. Visibility Settings Admin

Visibility lets you control exactly who can see a tour or an individual date. Dates (and tours) that a user cannot see are completely hidden from their list — they are not shown as locked or greyed out, they simply don't appear.

Admins always see everything. Visibility restrictions only apply to Tour Manager, Crew, and Artist roles.

Tour-Level Visibility

To restrict an entire tour, tap the eye icon on the tour's header row on the main Dates screen. The eye turns blue when a restriction is active. Choose Everyone or Restricted.

Tour-level visibility is the default for every date in that tour. If a date has no explicit visibility setting of its own, it inherits the tour restriction exactly — a user who can't see the tour won't see any of its dates either.

When you save a tour visibility change, any user who is now allowed at the tour level is automatically un-excluded from individual dates where they had previously been blocked. This means re-adding someone to a tour restores their access to all dates — you don't need to go through each date manually.

Date-Level Visibility (Override)

To override visibility for a single date, open the Show screen for that date and tap the eye icon in the header. The Date Visibility modal opens. Choose:

  • Everyone — all users can see this date, regardless of any tour-level restriction.
  • Restricted — only the selected groups and/or users can see this date, regardless of the tour-level setting.

Setting either option on a date marks it as explicitly set, which breaks the link to the tour-level default for that date. The rules are:

  • Date has no explicit setting → inherits the tour's visibility.
  • Date explicitly set to Everyone → visible to all users, even if the rest of the tour is restricted. Useful for adding a single date to someone not on the tour.
  • Date explicitly set to Restricted → only the people listed on that date can see it, regardless of tour visibility. Can be narrower or wider than the tour restriction.
Example: The tour is restricted to Band only. Open a specific date, set visibility to Restricted, and add your local crew member. That one date is now visible to them — the rest of the tour remains Band-only.
Example: The tour is restricted. Open a date, set it to Everyone, and it punches through the tour restriction — everyone can see that date even though the rest of the tour is hidden.
Date overrides are independent. Removing a user from a tour does not automatically remove their access to dates they were explicitly added to at the date level. If you want to remove someone from a specific date, open that date's visibility settings and untick them there. Conversely, re-adding someone at the tour level does automatically clear any date-level exclusions for them (see above).

Adding Someone to Specific Dates Without Joining the Tour

You can give a user access to one or more specific dates without adding them to the tour at all:

  1. Leave the tour visibility as Restricted (they are not in the tour's user list).
  2. Open each date you want them to see and set that date's visibility to Restricted, then add them individually.

The tour card will appear for them (because they have access to at least one date inside it), but they will only see the specific dates you have granted access to.

Selecting Groups & Users (Restricted mode)

When Restricted is chosen, the modal shows:

  • User Groups — select one or more groups. Every member of a selected group is granted access. When a group is selected, its members appear indented beneath it so you can exclude individuals (see below).
  • Individual Users — users not covered by any selected group. Tick to grant access.

A search bar at the top lets you find any user by name or email. When searching:

  • All matching users are shown in a single flat list, regardless of group membership.
  • Users covered by a selected group show via [Group Name] as their subtitle and are ticked blue. Tapping them toggles their exclusion from the group — untick to hide them even though they're in the group.
  • Users not in any selected group can be individually ticked or unticked as normal.

Tap Done to save. Tap Cancel to discard changes.

Per-Member Exclusions (Group Sub-list)

When a group is selected in the normal (non-search) view, its members appear indented beneath the group row, each with a blue tick. Untick any member to exclude that individual from this tour or date even though they are in the group — useful for keeping one person hidden without removing them from the group entirely. Their row dims to confirm they are excluded.

Visibility Indicator in the Dates List

A small eye icon appears on date rows in the Dates list whenever that date has a visibility restriction set. This icon is only visible to Admins and Tour Managers — Crew and Artists never see it.

Day Sheet

A Share Day Sheet button appears at the bottom of the Show screen for Admin and Tour Manager users. Tap it to generate a formatted Day Sheet document for the current date containing the day notes, a unified schedule (schedule items and transport entries sorted by time), the set list, and venue contacts.

Three format options are available:

  • Preview PDF — opens the native iOS print panel with a full paginated PDF preview. You can pinch-to-zoom and share directly from the preview using the share icon in the top-right corner.
  • Share PDF — generates the PDF and goes straight to the share sheet so you can send it via AirDrop, email, WhatsApp, etc.
  • Text File — generates a plain-text .txt file named day-sheet DD-MM-YYYY.txt (e.g. day-sheet 14-3-2026.txt) and opens the share sheet.
The Day Sheet PDF uses the organisation name as a header, followed by venue, address, tour, date, and city. Transport entries appear in the schedule in chronological order alongside regular schedule items — ground arrivals show a Maps link in the PDF. Venue contact emails are plain text in the PDF but are tappable links in the app.
PDF formatting: PDF rendering is subject to variation depending on device, OS version, PDF viewer, and the amount of content on the page. If the output isn't formatted to your liking — for example margins, line breaks, or page flow look off — use the Text File option instead and open the .txt file in any word processor or text editor to reformat and print as needed.

Day Notes

The Day Notes section near the top of the Show screen contains two separate note fields, each labelled with a coloured badge so readers know who wrote them:

  • Admin — visible to all users, but only editable by Admin. Use this for internal or management-level notes.
  • TM — visible to all users, editable by Admin and Tour Manager. Use this for day-of operational notes.

Both fields appear within the same Day Notes card. If a field is empty and the current user doesn't have permission to edit it, that field is hidden automatically. The notes content also appears at the top of the generated Day Sheet.

Saving

All editable fields on the Show Screen save automatically as you type — no save button required.

Auto-save triggers 1.5 seconds after you stop typing. If the device is offline, changes are stored locally and synced to the cloud when connectivity returns.
Tour Dates
Hotel

🏨Hotel

Access from the Hotel quick-action button on the Show screen. A show date can have multiple hotels — useful when band and crew are staying at different locations. Each hotel has its own name, address, notes, and room list.

Multiple Hotels

Hotels are displayed as collapsible sections. Tap a hotel header to expand or collapse it. The header shows the hotel name (or "Hotel 1", "Hotel 2", etc. until a name is entered).

Admin Tour Manager: Tap Add Hotel at the bottom of the screen to add another hotel for the same date. If more than one hotel exists, a trash icon appears in each hotel's header to remove it.

Hotel Details

Crew and Artist users can view hotel details but cannot edit them. The hotel name and full address are always displayed clearly for all users.

  • Hotel NameSearch field with Google Places autocomplete — editable by Admin Tour Manager only. Selecting a result automatically fills the Hotel Address. If the hotel doesn't appear in the search results, simply type the name directly — it is saved as entered.
  • Hotel AddressGoogle Places autocomplete search field — type to find and select an address. If the address isn't found in search results, type it manually. Auto-filled when a hotel name is selected from Google Places. Editable by Admin and Tour Manager.
  • Hotel NotesMultiline free-text — e.g. check-in instructions, parking notes. Editable by Admin and Tour Manager; visible to all.

Room Assignments

Each hotel has its own room list. Rooms are listed as collapsible cards — tap a card header to expand or collapse it. Each room has:

  • Name / GuestStart typing a name to search the crew list — matching crew members appear in a dropdown. Tap a name to fill the field instantly. You can also type any freeform name for non-crew guests.
  • Check Indd/mm/yyyy — auto-formatted as you type.
  • Check Outdd/mm/yyyy — auto-formatted as you type.
  • Room NumberThe hotel room or suite number. Admin Tour Manager can edit any room's number. Crew and Artist can edit the Room Number field on the room assigned to their own name.
  • Booking ReferenceConfirmation or booking reference code.
  • CostThe cost for this individual room. The currency is shared across all rooms in the same hotel and is set via the currency picker in the Room Costs panel. Editable by Admin Tour Manager only.
  • TaxTax amount for this room (e.g. GST, VAT). Optional — leave blank if not applicable.

Room Costs & Finance

Each hotel has a Room Costs panel above the room list. Tap the currency button (e.g. AUD) to open the currency picker — a full-screen modal with all major world currencies. A total summary appears automatically, summing all room costs and taxes. The currency applies to all rooms in that hotel.

Individual room Cost and Tax fields are shown in each room card. These fields are only visible to users with finance access.

When any room has a cost entered, the hotel is automatically added to the Finance screen as an Accommodation expense. The description includes the hotel name, address, and number of rooms (e.g. Grand Hyatt - 123 St - 3 rooms). The entry is removed automatically if all room costs are cleared.

Finance Visibility: The Room Costs panel and per-room cost fields are only visible to users with finance access. If Finance Visibility is set to Admin Only in Organisation Settings, Tour Managers will not see these fields. See Settings for more details.

Bulk Add Rooms

Each hotel has an Add Rooms panel. Set a Check In and Check Out date, then tap Add Users… to open the crew picker. Select individuals or entire groups and tap confirm — a room is created for each selected person with the dates pre-filled. People who already have a room in that hotel are skipped automatically.

Tap + Add Single Room (below the room list) to add one blank room manually.

Tap the trash icon on a room card to delete it.

Saving

All hotel fields save automatically as you type — no save button required. Tap the back arrow to return to the Show view at any time.

Show Screen
Transport

✈️Transport

Access from the Transport quick-action button on the Show screen. Records all travel for the day — flights and ground transport (bus, van, taxi, train, etc.).

Entries are listed in chronological order by departure time (earliest first). Entries without a departure time appear at the bottom.

Transport entries linked to a show date also appear in the Show screen's Schedule timeline alongside schedule items.

Transport Entry Types

  • Plane — flight with airline, flight number, departure/arrival airport codes, departure/arrival times, and optional seat info.
  • Ground — any non-flight leg (bus, van, taxi, train). Has departure and arrival name fields, optional notes, and a distance/duration route estimate.

Adding a Transport Entry Admin Tour Manager

  1. Tap + Add Flight or + Add Ground.
  2. Fill in the relevant fields (see below).
  3. Tap Save or navigate away — entries are saved automatically as you type.

Flight Fields

  • AirlineCarrier name (e.g. Qantas).
  • Flight Numbere.g. QF409.
  • Departure AirportIATA code (e.g. SYD).
  • Arrival AirportIATA code (e.g. MEL).
  • Departure TimeLocal departure time.
  • Arrival TimeLocal arrival time.
Tap the search icon next to the flight number to auto-fill airline, departure/arrival airports, and times using the live flight search feature. Once a flight number is entered, tap the blue flight number link ↗ to open a Google search for that flight's live status in your browser.

Ground Transport Fields

  • TypeBus, Van, Taxi, Train, Car, or Other.
  • DepartDeparture location name. Tap the blue pin icon to choose from known locations for this show date (venue, hotel, city) — see Location Quick-Pick below.
  • ArriveArrival location name. Same pin icon quick-pick available.
  • Departure TimeLocal time of departure.
  • NotesOptional free text (driver name, booking reference, etc.).

Location Quick-Pick

For ground transport entries, a blue pin icon appears next to the Depart and Arrive labels. Tapping it opens a bottom sheet showing known locations for this show date:

  • Venue — pulled from the show's venue name and address
  • Hotels — all hotels for this date (and the previous date) appear as individual options, including hotels entered manually without a Google Places selection
  • City — the show city as a fallback

Tap any location card to instantly fill the field. The route distance and duration are automatically recalculated after selection.

Route Calculation

When both Depart and Arrive fields are filled for a ground entry, the app calculates an estimated driving distance and duration using Google Maps and displays them on the entry card.

Flight Cost & Finance

For Plane entries, a Flight Cost section appears below the booking reference. Enter the cost and optional tax amount for the flight. Tap the currency button (e.g. AUD) to open the currency picker — a full-screen modal with all major world currencies. The current selection is highlighted with a checkmark.

When a flight cost is entered, the entry is automatically added to the Finance screen as a Travel expense. The description includes the party name, route (e.g. SYD → MEL), flight number, and booking reference. The entry is removed automatically if the cost is cleared or the transport entry is deleted.

🎟️Guest List

Access from the Guest List quick-action button on the Show screen. Manages the door list for a specific show.

Cutoff Time

A cutoff card is always shown at the top. Once the cutoff passes, Crew and Artist users can no longer add or modify guests and will see a red warning banner.

Admin Tour Manager can set or clear the cutoff at any time and are never blocked by it.

Setting the Cutoff

  1. Tap Set Cutoff (or Change if one is already set).
  2. On iOS: use the date spinner, tap Next, then set the time, tap Done.
  3. On Android: the native date and time pickers appear in sequence.
  4. On web: enter the date and time in text fields, then tap Done.

Guest Limit Admin

A maximum guest count can be set inside the cutoff card. Only Admin users can set or change this value.

  • Tap Set Limit and enter a number (e.g. 50), then tap Save. The counter immediately shows current / limit and turns red when the cap is reached.
  • Leave the field blank and tap Save to remove the limit.
  • Tour Manager users can see the current / limit counter but cannot change the limit.
  • When the limit is reached, any attempt to add another guest will be blocked with an alert — regardless of role.
The guest limit counts total guests across all entries, taking the Quantity field into account. One entry with Qty 3 counts as three guests against the limit.

Adding Guests

  1. Tap the Add Guest button below the guest list.
  2. A form slides up from the bottom. Fill in First Name, Last Name, Quantity (defaults to 1), and optional Notes (e.g. VIP, AAA, Photo Pass), Phone, and Email.
  3. Tap Add Guest to save, or tap outside the form or the ✕ button to dismiss without saving.

Each guest card shows the name, quantity, notes, and any phone/email details entered. Below the name, a Requested by line shows the display name of the user who added the guest, along with the date they were added (Requested on). Both fields are set automatically — they are not entered manually. The running total is shown at the top of the list.

Approving & Denying Guests Admin Tour Manager

Every guest card has two status pill buttons on the right side:

  • Approve — tap to mark the guest as approved (green pill, green left border on the card). Tap again to revert to pending.
  • Deny — tap to mark the guest as denied (red pill, red left border, card dimmed). Tap again to revert to pending.

Approve and Deny are mutually exclusive — setting one clears the other. Crew and Artist users see a read-only pill showing Approved, Denied, or Pending and cannot change it.

The running approved count is shown in two places: below the guest list heading on the left, and as an Approved: N total on the right (next to the overall Total). Both update immediately as you approve or deny guests. The right-side total reflects quantities, so a guest with a quantity of 2 counts as 2.

Approve All

Admin Tour Manager: Tap the Approve All checkbox in the guest list header to approve every guest at once. Tap it again to unapprove all.

Editing & Deleting Guests

Tap the pencil icon on a guest card to open the edit form in the same bottom-sheet modal. Make your changes, then tap Update Guest to save or Cancel to discard. Tap the trash icon to delete a guest (confirmation required).

Artist users can also edit and delete guests that they personally added — pencil and trash icons appear on their own entries only.

Exporting the Guest List Admin Tour Manager

The Export Guest List button appears when at least one guest has been added. Tapping it generates a PDF of approved guests only, sorted by surname, with separate Surname and First Name columns followed by quantity, notes, phone, email, and who requested each guest.

On iOS and Android the PDF opens in the in-app viewer where you can pinch-to-zoom and tap Share to send via AirDrop, email, or any other share option. On web, the browser print dialog opens instead.
